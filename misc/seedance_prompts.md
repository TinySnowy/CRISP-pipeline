## Scene 1: The Switchback Stairs

You are an industrial motion-capture and 3D geometric data synthesizer. Your objective is to generate physically flawless, temporally stable video data for computer vision extraction and robotics simulation. Visual Style: Clinical engineering test footage. Photorealistic, bright even diffuse lighting with no harsh shadows, sharp focus at all depths, zero motion blur, zero cinematic depth-of-field. Camera: Mounted on a perfectly stabilized mechanical dolly at 1.5–2 meters from the subject so the subject fills at least half the frame height. Always moves with a lateral or diagonal component — never purely forward — to ensure scene depth can be triangulated. No handheld shake, no zooming, no focal length changes. The Subject: Human in tight-fitting solid crimson athletic wear. No loose clothing. Entire body from head to toe fully visible in frame at all times. The Environment: Enclosed or semi-enclosed space — walls on both sides and ceiling or overhang above so the entire frame is filled with trackable surfaces. No open sky visible. Rigid man-made planar geometry only. Dense high-contrast repeating surface textures. No organic geometry, no featureless blank walls. Physics: Slow deliberate weight-bearing movement, physically accurate foot contact, zero foot sliding, at least one foot on the ground at all times. Duration: 8–12 seconds, no cuts.

The subject walks slowly down a set of concrete switchback stairs inside an urban parking garage. The stair treads are bare concrete with a non-slip ribbed texture. The walls are cinderblock painted grey. A low concrete ceiling is visible above. No open sky. The camera dollies diagonally — tracking downward and slightly to the side — at the same speed as the subject, keeping them centered in frame and revealing the receding depth of the staircase behind them. The subject does not touch any walls or railings. Full body visible at all times.
